Big Cat Rescue

Problem

How can we redesign Big Cat Rescue to help retain and expand their membership base?

Objective

Big Cat Rescue should educate their users about their mission in a manner that would convince visitors to donate towards the cause.

Solution

The site was redesigned to convey a vibrant and exotic style to all animal lovers. With an attractive interface and fun new interactions, it’s a surefire way to increase site traffic and donations.

Figma Mobile Prototype

figma.fun/nCp4LB

Role

UX Researcher, Design System Lead, Graphic Designer, Figma Prototype Artist

Tools

Figma, Photoshop

Timeline

3 Weeks

User Research

We conducted surveys and user interviews and learned that our typical user was between 30-35 and a likely pet owner.
They have donated in the past and prefer to be referred by a close friend or relative.
They would only like an annual update after donating and organizational transparency is critical.

Definition

User Goals

  • Feel they can trust the organization
  • Know how their donations will be used
  • Want more payment options to donate
  • Avoid information overload

Feature Priorities

  • Option to pay via Venmo, Zelle or Credit Card
  • Option to donate once or monthly
  • Offer newsletter subscription
  • Interactive way for users to learn about the type of cats in the sanctuary

Future Opportunities

With more time for research and development, our team would like to provide various iterations to stakeholders. We would also like to conduct more user tests to find other ways to entice users to donate.

Feastly

Problem

Users need a quick, secure and hassle-free way to order and pay quickly at fast casual restaurants.

Objective

Build a fully branded mobile prototype with CSS Grid.

Solution

An app that features a simple and intuitive menu system that makes it easy for customers to place and customize food orders as soon as they are seated.

Mobile Prototype

ricktorresjunior.github.io/Feastly

Role

Front-End Developer, UX Researcher, Graphic Designer

Tools

HTML & CSS, Figma, Photoshop

Timeline

3 Weeks

User Research

We conducted surveys and user interviews and learned that 80% of our users preferred QR menus to paper menus.
60% enjoy interacting with servers or other restaurant staff.
70% have altered or substituted an ingredient.
90% are comfortable linking their credit cards to their phones via Apple Pay or other services.

Definition

User Goals

  • Want an easy way to order and pay at restaurants
  • Want the option to substitute ingredients on the app
  • Want multiple payment options
  • Avoid scrolling through long or complicated menus

Feature Priorities

  • Option to add food allergies to user profile
  • Option to pay via Credit Card, Apple Pay or cash
  • Intuitive and easy to use menu with sections for food and drinks
  • Ability to add restaurant to Favorites
